What Is Your Carbon Footprint?

Your carbon footprint is the amount of CO2 (carbon dioxide) and other carbon substances emitted through the use of fossil fuels by you and/or a group of people. Essentially everyday of your life, you are contributing to the increasing amount of carbon and greenhouse gases in our atmosphere, which contributes to global warming and climate change. This includes activites such as driving a car, riding the bus, or heating your house.

Luckily, you are in control of reducing your carbon footprint! You can use the web app to easily calculate your carbon footprint and discover ways to mitigate your enviornmental impact.

Reducing Your Carbon Footprint

Reduce the amount you drive

Try taking alternatives such as the train, bus, biking, or walking. Reduce unnecessary acceleration. Avoid traffic as waiting out on the road produces more CO2.

Reduce your energy use at home

Make sure your home is properly insulated to keep heat in. Purchase ENERGY STAR appliances. Use natural lighting by opening your curtains.

Change the food you eat

Eat locally grown and organic food. Reduce your meat and dairy intake.

Extra ways to reduce

Reduce your water usage. Take shorter showers, don't leave the sink on while brushing your teeth, install dual-flush toilets, etc. Reduce, reuse, and recycle. Build a rooftop garden (or a reuglar one). Reuse storm/graywater to irrigate plants. Install and programmable thermostat.
